Wyoming Blockchain Legislation


HB 19 The Wyoming Money Transmitter Act-virtual currency exemption

provides an exemption for virtual currency (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.) used within Wyoming from money transmitter laws and regulations

HB 70 Utility Token Bill

provides that a person who develops, sells or facilitates the exchange of an open blockchain token (a “consumer” or “utility” token) is not subject to specified securities and money transmission laws

SF 111 The Cryptocurrency Property Tax Exemption Bill

provides that virtual currency is not subject to taxation as “property” in Wyoming.

HB 101 The Blockchain Records Bill

 provides for the maintenance of corporate records of Wyoming entities via blockchain so long as electronic keys, network signatures and digital receipts are used.

HB 126 Limited liability companies-series

modifies Wyoming’s corporate code to permit the formation of “Series LLCs.” The intent of this legislation is to promote Wyoming as a jurisdiction of choice for business formation.





Wyoming Benefits

The subsidiary companies of WYO.LLC are afforded unparalleled opportunities because of the Wyoming business environment enabled by the new legislation.

Privacy Preservation

The reporting and disclosure requirements for Wyoming companies are very minimal, and Wyoming LLC ownership is not maintained on public record.

Lack of red tape

Wyoming believes in a philosophy of independence, innovation, and technological progress. As a result, the state legislature lets businesses conduct themselves without much government overreach or overregulation of industries.

Favorable tax environment

Wyoming does not have a state income tax, franchise tax, or taxes on corporate shares.

Lobbying opportunities

Due to the smaller population of the state, companies have many opportunities and avenues to interact with politicians and positively impact public policy for their businesses.









Heritage

Wyoming has a long tradition of combining innovation and business. We were the home of the first LLC in 1977, a decade before the IRS pursued any similar measures and 19 years before they issued the final ruling that we use today. Wyoming had the first business west of the Missouri River, first county public library system, and the first woman governor. It has always been a place that attracts innovative business people and ideas.


Now, Wyoming can also be seen leading the charge in blockchain and cryptocurrency technology. The Wyoming legislature has developed a favorable regulatory environment for Series LLCs, virtual currencies, and innovation. Combining this with the favorable business climate in Wyoming makes it the prefect place to bring your startup.
